## Why this site exists

This site is not meant to be a static CV. A CV is useful, but it compresses too much into job titles, bullet points, and technology lists.

I want this space to work as a living index of what I am building, studying, and testing. Some projects are polished. Some are prototypes. Some are research threads that may become useful later.

## The direction

The common thread is practical engineering around backend systems, applied AI, developer tools, and knowledge workflows.

I am especially interested in systems that connect different layers:

- raw data and decision-making
- documents and semantic search
- LLMs and deterministic tooling
- domain knowledge and usable software
- prototypes and real workflows

## How I want to use it

The plan is to keep projects visible, write short lab notes, and make my technical direction easier to understand from the outside.

If a project changes, the site should change with it.
