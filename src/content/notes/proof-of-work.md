## The principle

I prefer proof of work over static claims.

It is easy to list technologies. It is more useful to show what was built with them, why certain choices were made, and what tradeoffs appeared along the way.

## What counts as proof

For this portfolio, proof of work means:

- working code
- reproducible pipelines
- clear documentation
- demos or published reports when possible
- explicit limitations
- technical decisions that can be inspected

## Why it matters

This is especially important for AI-related work. A polished demo can hide weak assumptions. A useful system should make its inputs, uncertainty, constraints, and failure modes easier to reason about.

That is the kind of software I want to keep building.
